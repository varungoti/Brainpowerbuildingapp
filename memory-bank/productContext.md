# Product Context

## Why this project exists
Parents need daily, practical, development-supportive activities that are easier to use than static worksheets, safer than generic AI advice, and more adaptive than one-size-fits-all content.

## Problems being solved
- Parents struggle to turn developmental research into daily action.
- Most activity catalogs are not personalized to age, mood, time, materials, or prior activity history.
- Family products often over-collect data or make unsafe claims.
- AI-era parenting guidance is fragmented and can drift into hype or medical overreach.

## Intended user experience
- A parent can quickly sign in or stay local-first.
- The app generates a useful, varied activity pack in seconds.
- Parents understand why an activity was chosen and can complete/log it easily.
- Trust is reinforced through legal/privacy transparency, restrained analytics, and clear AI boundaries.

## Differentiation direction
- Strong evidence/safety guardrails.
- Personalization plus diversity constraints.
- Longitudinal family value from outcomes, history, and curriculum-like progression.
- Parent-facing clarity rather than gimmicky “brain training” claims.
- Provider-neutral prompt/media orchestration should stay review-gated and metadata-driven, never auto-publishing.
